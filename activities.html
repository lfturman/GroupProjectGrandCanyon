Discover Your Grand Canyon Adventure

The Grand Canyon offers unforgettable experiences for every type of traveler. This 277-mile geological wonder carved by the Colorado River features activities ranging from challenging rim-to-rim hikes and whitewater rafting to scenic drives and peaceful sunrise viewpoints. Whether you have a day or a week, there's an adventure to match your interests and fitness level.

Hiking

The Grand Canyon is an unrivaled destination for avid hikers, with a variety of trails to choose from â€“ each offering stunning views. Popular trails include the Bright Angel Trail, South Kaibab Trail, and the challenging rim-to-rim hike. Whether you're looking for a short day hike or a multi-day backcountry adventure, the canyon's diverse terrain provides options for all skill levels.

River Rafting

Experience the Grand Canyon from a completely different perspective on a Colorado River rafting trip. Navigate thrilling rapids, camp under starlit skies, and discover hidden waterfalls and side canyons accessible only by water. Trips range from half-day smooth water floats to multi-week whitewater expeditions through the heart of the canyon.

Scenic Drives & Viewpoints

For those who prefer a more relaxed experience, the Grand Canyon's scenic drives offer breathtaking vistas accessible by car. Desert View Drive along the South Rim features numerous pullouts and viewpoints, each offering a unique perspective of the canyon. Don't miss sunrise or sunset at popular spots like Mather Point, Yavapai Point, or Desert View Watchtower.

Mule Rides

Step back in time with a traditional mule ride into the canyon. These guided tours offer a unique way to descend into the canyon while learning about its history and geology from experienced wranglers. Day trips to Plateau Point or overnight stays at Phantom Ranch are available, providing an unforgettable Old West adventure.

Camping

Immerse yourself in the canyon's natural beauty with a camping experience. The South Rim offers several developed campgrounds with amenities, while adventurous souls can obtain backcountry permits for primitive camping below the rim. Wake up to stunning sunrise views and fall asleep under some of the darkest, most star-filled skies in the country.

Photography

The Grand Canyon is a photographer's paradise, offering endless opportunities to capture its majesty. The interplay of light and shadow throughout the day creates constantly changing scenes, with golden hour and blue hour providing particularly magical lighting. From wide landscape shots to intimate details of the canyon's geology and wildlife, every visit yields new photographic treasures.
