Frequently Asked Questions

Everything you need to know about visiting the Grand Canyon

General Information

How deep is the Grand Canyon?

The Grand Canyon is approximately 1 mile (1.6 km) deep at its deepest point. The average depth is about 1 mile, with the canyon stretching 277 miles long and up to 18 miles wide.

How old is the Grand Canyon?

The rocks at the bottom of the Grand Canyon are nearly 2 billion years old, but the canyon itself was carved by the Colorado River over the past 5 to 6 million years.

Which rim should I visit - North or South?

The South Rim is open year-round and is the most visited, with more facilities and viewpoints. The North Rim is higher in elevation, less crowded, and offers a different perspective, but is only open mid-May through mid-October due to snow.

Planning Your Visit

What is the best time to visit?

Spring (March-May) and fall (September-November) offer the best weather with mild temperatures and fewer crowds. Summer can be very hot and crowded, while winter offers fewer visitors but cold temperatures and possible snow.

How much does it cost to enter?

The entrance fee is $35 per vehicle for a 7-day pass, $30 per motorcycle, or $20 per person for those entering on foot or bicycle. An annual pass costs $70.

How long should I plan to visit?

For a first visit to the South Rim, plan at least one full day. Two to three days allows time for hiking, watching sunrise/sunset, and exploring multiple viewpoints. If you want to hike to the bottom or visit both rims, plan 4-5 days.

Do I need reservations?

You don't need reservations to enter the park, but lodging inside the park should be booked 6-12 months in advance, especially for summer. Backcountry camping permits require reservations and can be obtained up to 4 months in advance.

Hiking & Safety

Can I hike to the bottom and back in one day?

The National Park Service strongly discourages hiking to the river and back in one day. It's extremely strenuous (16-19 miles round trip with significant elevation change) and dangerous, especially in summer. Many experienced hikers have required rescue attempting this.

What should I bring for a day hike?

Bring plenty of water (at least 1 liter per 2 hours of hiking), salty snacks, sun protection (hat, sunscreen, sunglasses), a first aid kit, and a map. Wear sturdy hiking boots and layers of clothing. Start early to avoid afternoon heat.

Are there guardrails everywhere?

No. While major viewpoints have railings, many areas along the rim do not. Stay on designated trails and at least 6 feet from the edge. Deaths from falls occur every year, so always be cautious and supervise children closely.

Wildlife & Nature

What animals might I see?

Common wildlife includes mule deer, elk, California condors, ravens, squirrels, and various lizards. Mountain lions and rattlesnakes are present but rarely seen. Never feed or approach wildlife, and maintain a safe distance.

Is altitude a concern?

The South Rim sits at 7,000 feet elevation and the North Rim at 8,000 feet. Some visitors may experience mild altitude sickness. Stay hydrated, take it easy the first day, and be aware of symptoms like headache, nausea, or dizziness.
